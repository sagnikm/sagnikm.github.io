Biography

I am a Lecturer in Algorithms in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Sheffield. In my previous avatar, I was a post-doctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science in the University of Copenhagen in 2021, at KTH Royal Institute of Technology during 2019-2021, a post-doctoral fellow at IUUK, Charles University in 2018, in the APC group at KTH Royal Institute of Technology during 2017-2018, and a graduate student in theoretical computer science at TIFR Mumbai until August 2017, working under the guidance of Dr. Arkadev Chattopadhyay.

I was a recipient of TCS Ph.D. Fellowship and my Ph.D. research work was supported by this fellowship.

July 2022 In FOCS 2022
Cut Query Algorithms with Star Contraction

We study the complexity of determining the edge connectivity of a simple graph with cut queries. We show that (i) there is a bounded-error randomized algorithm that computes edge connectivity with $O(n)$ cut queries, and (ii) there is a bounded-error quantum algorithm that computes edge connectivity with $\tilde O(\sqrt{n})$ cut queries.
Joakim Blikstad, Jan van den Brand, Yuval Efron, Sagnik Mukhopadhyay, Danupon Nanongkai
July 2022 In FOCS 2022
Nearly Optimal Communication and Query Complexity of Bipartite Matching

We settle the complexities of the maximum-cardinality bipartite matching problem (BMM) up to poly-logarithmic factors in five models of computation: the two-party communication, AND query, OR query, XOR query, and quantum edge query models.
Joakim Blikstad, Jan van den Brand, Sagnik Mukhopadhyay, Danupon Nanongkai
January 2021 In STOC 2021
Breaking the Quadratic Barrier for Matroid Intersection

The matroid intersection problem is a fundamental problem that has been extensively studied for half a century. In the classic version of this problem, we are given two matroids ${\cal M}_1 = (V, {\cal I}_1)$ and ${\cal M}_2 = (V, {\cal I}_2)$ on a comment ground set $V$ of $n$ elements, and then we have to find the largest common independent set $S \in {\cal I}_1 \cap {\cal I}_2$ by making independence oracle queries of the form ‘‘Is $S \in {\cal I}_1$?
Sagnik Mukhopadhyay, Danupon Nanongkai
January 2020 In STOC 2020
Weighted Min-Cut: Sequential, Cut-Query and Streaming Algorithms

Consider the following 2-respecting min-cut problem. Given a weighted graph $G$ and its spanning tree $T$, find the minimum cut among the cuts that contain at most two edges in $T$.
Arkadev Chattopadhyay, Michal Koucký, Bruno Loff, Sagnik Mukhopadhyay
July 2018 In STOC 2018
Simulation Beats Richness: New Data-Structure Lower Bounds

We develop a technique for proving lower bounds in the setting of asymmetric communication, a model that was introduced in the famous works of Miltersen (STOC'94) and Miltersen, Nisan, Safra and Wigderson (STOC'95).
